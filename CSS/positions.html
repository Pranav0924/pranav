Virat Kohli

About Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli, an elite cricketer, debuted for India in 2008. Renowned for his aggressive yet technically sound batting, he has excelled across formats, amassing over 8,000 Test runs, 12,000 ODI runs, and over 4,000 T20I runs. Kohli captained the Indian team from 2017 to 2021, leading them to significant victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia. With numerous centuries and records to his name, he is celebrated for his consistency and leadership. His impact extends to the IPL, where he has been a key player for the Royal Challengers Bangalore.

virat kohli(T20)

Virat Kohli, a premier T20I batsman, debuted for India in 2009 and retired from the format in 2022. Over his career, he scored over 4000 runs at an average above 50, marked by numerous fifties and several centuries. Kohli captained India from 2017 to 2021, leading them to notable victories and achieving the top ICC T20I team ranking. His aggressive yet technically sound batting and ability to perform in crucial moments established him as one of the format's greats, leaving a lasting impact on T20I cricket.

virat kohli(ODI)

Virat Kohli, debuting in ODIs in 2008, has become one of the format’s most prolific batsmen. With over 12,000 runs at an average exceeding 50, he ranks among the top run-scorers in ODI history. Kohli has scored numerous centuries and fifties, demonstrating exceptional consistency and adaptability. As captain from 2017 to 2021, he led India to significant victories and held the top ICC ODI team ranking. Renowned for his aggressive yet technically precise batting, Kohli has had a profound impact on the game, contributing immensely to India’s success in One Day Internationals.

virat kohli(TEST)

Virat Kohli, who debuted in Test cricket in 2008, is one of the format's premier batsmen. With over 8,000 runs at an average above 50, he is renowned for his aggressive and technically refined style. Kohli has scored numerous centuries, including several double centuries, and holds multiple records for quick milestones. He captained India from 2014 to 2021, leading them to notable series wins, including their historic triumph in Australia in 2020-21. His contributions have significantly shaped India's success in Test cricket, cementing his status as one of the all-time greats in the format.

virat kohli(RCB)

Virat Kohli's IPL career with the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) began in 2008. He captained the team from 2013 to 2021, becoming one of the league's top run-scorers with over 6,000 runs. Kohli set the record for the most runs in a single season (973 runs in 2016) and scored four centuries in that year. Despite his stellar individual performances and consistent form, RCB did not win an IPL title during his captaincy. Kohli's leadership and batting made him a central figure in RCB’s history, significantly shaping the team's fortunes over his tenure.
